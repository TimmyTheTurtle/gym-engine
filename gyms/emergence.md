# Emergence Gym

## Purpose

Emergence Gym is the track for systems in which complex behavior arises from simple local rules, repeated interactions, or iterative feedback.

This Gym fits experiments that feel alive, self-organizing, surprising, or structurally rich.

This Gym is a **conceptual collection**, not a code ownership container.

Experiments remain implemented in `/experiments` and may also belong to other Gyms.

---

## What This Gym Trains

This Gym develops intuition in:

- self-organization
- swarm behavior
- local-to-global effects
- pattern formation
- nonlinear dynamics
- iterative rule systems
- feedback loops
- evolutionary dynamics
- procedural growth
- multi-agent coordination

It emphasizes emergence as something to be:
- observed
- perturbed
- iterated
- explored through rules and parameters
- appreciated as structure arising from simplicity

---

## Core Learning Style

This Gym teaches through visible systems such as:

- flocks
- reacting fields
- growing structures
- caves
- swarms
- cellular worlds
- evolving populations
- branching forms

The learner should repeatedly see that small rules can generate large-scale order or complexity.

---

## Relationship to Experiments

Important rule:

Experiments are the atomic runnable units.  
This Gym is a curated view over experiments that train emergence intuition.

An experiment may belong to multiple Gyms.

Examples:
- `reaction_diffusion` belongs here, but also belongs to Simulation and Prueitt Mathematics
- `flocking` belongs here, but also belongs to AI & Learning
- `plant_growth_lsystem` belongs here, but also belongs to Algorithms & Geometry and Simulation

Do not treat this Gym as a code module.

## Inclusion Rule

A new experiment belongs in this Gym if it significantly trains one or more of:

- local-to-global reasoning
- pattern formation
- iterative complexity
- self-organization
- multi-agent coordination
- rule-driven growth or adaptation

and if the resulting system is visually interesting to observe over time.

---

## Anti-Patterns

Incorrect:
- treating emergence as just a subtype of simulation
- requiring biology-like subject matter
- assuming emergent experiments must be isolated from AI or Algorithms

Correct:
- let Emergence Gym overlap strongly with Simulation, AI, and Algorithms
- include systems whose key lesson is “complexity from simple rules”
- preserve the experiment-first structure
