# Algorithms & Geometry Gym

## Purpose

Algorithms & Geometry Gym is the track for visualizing core computer science ideas such as search, recursion, computational geometry, and procedural construction.

This Gym focuses on experiments where the logic of the algorithm is made visible.

This Gym is a **conceptual collection**, not a code ownership container.

Experiments remain implemented in `/experiments` and may also belong to other Gyms.

---

## What This Gym Trains

This Gym develops intuition in:

- recursion
- graph traversal
- pathfinding
- search frontiers
- computational geometry
- procedural construction
- spatial reasoning
- geometric transformation
- growth grammars
- algorithm behavior over time

It emphasizes algorithms as something to be:
- watched
- stepped
- compared
- explored through structure and motion
- understood through visible intermediate states

---

## Core Learning Style

This Gym teaches through visual systems such as:

- pathfinding waves
- maze generation
- geometric partitions
- transform-based structure
- recursively branching systems
- procedural diagrams

The learner should see what the algorithm is doing, not just what answer it returns.

---

## Relationship to Experiments

Important rule:

Experiments are the atomic runnable units.  
This Gym is a curated view over experiments that train algorithmic and geometric intuition.

An experiment may belong to multiple Gyms.

Examples:
- `maze_pathfinding` belongs here, but also belongs to AI & Learning
- `transform_rosettes` belongs here, but also belongs to Prueitt Mathematics
- `plant_growth_lsystem` belongs here, but also belongs to Emergence and Simulation

Do not treat this Gym as a code module.

## Inclusion Rule

A new experiment belongs in this Gym if it significantly trains one or more of:

- algorithm structure
- search
- recursion
- spatial reasoning
- procedural geometry
- visible intermediate computation

and if the learner can understand the algorithm better by watching it unfold.

---

## Anti-Patterns

Incorrect:
- treating algorithm experiments as dry, non-visual modules
- assuming this Gym cannot overlap with AI, Math, or Emergence
- organizing experiments into Gym-owned source folders

Correct:
- keep algorithm experiments highly visual
- allow overlap with AI, Mathematics, and Emergence
- use this Gym as a conceptual training lens, not a code boundary
